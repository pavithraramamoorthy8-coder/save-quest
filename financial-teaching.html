Module 1: Foundational Budgeting

1.1 The 50/30/20 Rule

This is a simple, effective budgeting method for managing your monthly income after taxes (Net Income). It provides a framework for allocating funds to different spending categories to ensure financial stability.

Allocation Breakdown

  • 50% Needs: Essential living expenses (Rent, Food, Minimum debt payments).
  • 30% Wants: Non-essential spending (Entertainment, Dining out, Hobbies).
  • 20% Savings/Debt: Financial goals (Emergency fund, Investments, Extra debt payments).

1.2 Tracking Expenses

Accurate tracking is non-negotiable for effective budgeting. Use apps, spreadsheets, or physical notebooks to monitor every dollar spent. This highlights where your money is actually going versus where you *think* it's going.

Module 2: Wealth Generation

2.1 The Power of Compounding

Compounding interest is interest earned on previous interest. This allows your wealth to grow exponentially over time. It is the single most powerful force in long-term investing, making early saving crucial. [attachment_0](attachment)

Formula Fact

The **future value (FV)** of a compounded investment can be calculated using the formula $FV = P(1 + r/n)^{nt}$, where P is the principal, r is the annual interest rate, n is the number of times interest is compounded per year, and t is the time in years.

2.2 Investment Diversification

Never put all your capital into a single asset. Diversification means spreading investments across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate) to reduce risk. When one sector performs poorly, another may perform well, balancing your overall portfolio.

Module 3: Debt and Credit Management

3.1 Understanding Credit Score

Your credit score (e.g., FICO or VantageScore) is a numeric representation of your creditworthiness. A higher score unlocks better interest rates for loans, mortgages, and credit cards. It is primarily built on payment history and credit utilization.

3.2 Debt Reduction Strategies

Two popular methods for tackling high-interest debt:

  • Debt Snowball: Pay off the smallest debts first for motivational victories, regardless of the interest rate.
  • Debt Avalanche: Pay off the debt with the highest interest rate first, saving the most money in the long run.
