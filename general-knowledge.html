Historical Epochs & Events

1.1 The Industrial Revolution (c. 1760 – 1840)

This period marked a transition to new manufacturing processes, transforming rural, agrarian societies into industrial, urban ones. Key innovations included the steam engine and mechanized textile production.

Key Innovation: The Steam Engine

Impact: Patented by James Watt, the improved steam engine provided a powerful, reliable, and portable source of energy, moving industry away from water power and allowing for the growth of factories in diverse locations.

1.2 The Renaissance (c. 14th – 16th Century)

A fervent period of European cultural, artistic, political, and economic "rebirth" following the Middle Ages. It emphasized Humanism, a focus on human values and capabilities rather than divine matters.

World Geography and Earth Science

2.1 Earth's Interior Structure

The Earth's structure is typically described in four main layers: the crust, mantle, outer core, and inner core. Understanding these layers is fundamental to explaining geological phenomena like plate tectonics. [attachment_0](attachment)

Layer Composition Fact

The **Inner Core** is a solid sphere of iron and nickel, despite being the hottest layer, due to the immense pressure preventing the atoms from moving into a liquid state.

2.2 The Seven Continents

The continents are commonly listed as: Asia (the largest), Africa, North America, South America, Antarctica, Europe, and Australia (the smallest). Continent definitions can vary based on geographic and cultural factors (e.g., Afro-Eurasia).

Science & Global Affairs

3.1 The Human Circulatory System

The circulatory system is responsible for transporting oxygen and nutrients to the body's tissues and removing carbon dioxide and waste. It comprises the heart, blood vessels (arteries, veins, capillaries), and blood. [attachment_1](attachment)

3.2 Global Economic Organizations

Organizations like the **IMF (International Monetary Fund)** and the **World Bank** play vital roles in regulating global finance, providing loans, and promoting economic stability among member nations.
