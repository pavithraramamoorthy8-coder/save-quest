Module 1: Physics & Universal Laws

1.1 Newton's Laws of Motion

These three laws form the foundation of classical mechanics, describing the relationship between a body and the forces acting upon it, and the body's motion in response to those forces.

The Second Law: Force and Acceleration

This law states that the acceleration ($a$) of an object is dependent upon the net force ($F$) acting upon the object and the mass ($m$) of the object. [attachment_0](attachment) This relationship is expressed by the equation: $F = ma$.

1.2 Energy Conservation

The Law of Conservation of Energy states that energy can neither be created nor destroyed; rather, it can only be transformed from one form (e.g., kinetic, potential, thermal) into another. This principle underlies all physical and chemical processes.

Module 2: Computing and Data

2.1 The Binary System

All modern computers operate using the binary numeral system, which represents data using only two digits: 0 (off) and 1 (on). This forms the basic language of all digital information, from text to video. [attachment_1](attachment)

Data Unit Hierarchy

8 bits = 1 Byte. A Byte is the smallest unit of data typically used to represent a character (like 'A' or '1').

2.2 What is an Algorithm?

An algorithm is a step-by-step procedure or formula for solving a problem, especially by a computer. Algorithms are essential to all software, from simple math calculations to complex AI decision-making.

This is the process by which organisms combine oxygen with food molecules (like glucose), diverting the chemical energy in these substances into life-sustaining activities and releasing carbon dioxide as waste.
